## How to Challenge

**Rule refs:** 4.6

A challenge is combat between your character and an opponent's exerted character OR an opponent's location.

### Process (Step by Step)

1. **Choose Challenging Character** - Your dry character
2. **Choose Target** - Opponent's exerted character OR location
3. **Check Restrictions** - Must be able to challenge target
4. **Challenge Damage Step** - Each deals Ⓒ damage to other simultaneously

### During Main Phase

- Can challenge multiple times per turn
- Each challenge is separate turn action
- Must complete one challenge before starting another

---

## Valid Targets

**Rule refs:** 4.6.2

### Challenging Character Requirements

- Must be **dry** (in play at start of your turn)
- Must not be exerted
- Must be able to choose target (see restrictions)

### Challenged Character Requirements

- Must be **exerted** (opponent's character)
- OR must be a location

### Can't Challenge

- Drying character (played after start of turn)
- Already exerted character
- Character restricted by keywords/effects

## Challenge Restrictions

**Rule refs:** 4.6.5, 8.2-8.8

Several keywords and abilities create challenge restrictions.

### Evasive (8.6)

**Restriction:** "This [Type] can't be challenged except by a character with Evasive."

**What it means:**

- Normal characters can't challenge Evasive characters
- Evasive characters can challenge Evasive characters
- No restriction on challenging non-Evasive characters

### Alert (8.2)

**Effect:** "This character ignores the challenging restriction of Evasive."

**What it means:**

- Alert character CAN challenge Evasive characters
- Doesn't gain Evasive, just ignores restriction

### Bodyguard (8.3)

**Restriction:** "If an opponent would choose one of your characters to challenge, they must choose this character or another character with Bodyguard if able."

**What it means:**

- Opponent must challenge Bodyguard character if possible
- Can choose among multiple Bodyguards
- Only applies if Bodyguard can be challenged (not Evasive, not exerted, etc.)

### Ward (8.15)

**Restriction:** "Your opponents can't choose this card when resolving an effect."

**What it means:**

- Opponents can't choose Ward character as challenge target
- Effects that don't choose still work (e.g., "all opposing characters")

### Reckless (8.7)

**Turn-end restriction:** "You can't declare the end of your turn if this character is ready and can challenge an opposing character or location."

**What it means:**

- Can't end turn while ready Reckless character could challenge
- Character must be exerted or unable to challenge to end turn

## Challenge Damage

**Rule refs:** 4.6.3-4.6.4

During Challenge Damage Step:

### Character vs Character

1. Challenging character deals Ⓒ damage to challenged
2. Challenged character deals Ⓒ damage to challenging
3. Damage happens simultaneously
4. Damage modifiers (Resist, Challenger, etc.) apply before damage dealt

### Character vs Location

1. Challenging character deals Ⓒ damage to location
2. Location deals NO damage back
3. Location has ⣇ (willpower) like characters

### Damage Calculation

- Start with character's printed Ⓒ
- Apply all modifiers (Challenger +N, negative Ⓒ, etc.)
- Final Ⓒ = damage dealt
- Resist reduces damage dealt

### Simultaneous Damage

- Both characters deal damage at same time
- If both would be banished, both are
- Triggers from banishment see both characters leaving

## Keywords in Challenges

**Rule refs:** 8.5-8.9

### Challenger +N (8.5)

"While this character is challenging, they gain +N Ⓒ."

- Applies ONLY while challenging
- Stacks with other +N effects
- Doesn't apply when being challenged

### Rush (8.9)

"This character can challenge as though they were in play at the beginning of your turn."

- Allows challenging while drying
- Character still drying for other purposes (can't quest, pay costs)
- Only allows challenging, not other dry-only actions

### Resist +N (8.8)

"Damage that would be dealt to this character or location is reduced by N."

- Reduces damage dealt to this card
- Doesn't affect damage counters put onto card
- Stacks with other Resist effects

### Evasive (8.6)

"This [Type] can't be challenged except by a character with Evasive."

- Creates targeting restriction
- Alert ignores this restriction

### Support (8.13)

"Whenever this character quests, you may add this character's Ⓒ to another chosen character's Ⓒ this turn."

- Adds Ⓒ to another character for rest of turn
- Triggered from questing, not challenging
- Ⓒ boost applies during challenges too

## Challenging Locations

**Rule refs:** 5.6.6

Locations can be challenged like characters but with differences.

### How Location Challenges Work

1. Challenging character deals Ⓒ damage to location
2. Location deals NO damage back
3. Location has ⣇ (willpower) - banished when damage ≥ ⣇

### Location Characteristics

- Has ⣇ (willpower) but no Ⓒ
- May have ◇ (lore value)
- May have move cost (◊)
- May have activated abilities

### Challenge Location vs Character

- **Character vs Character:** Both deal damage to each other
- **Character vs Location:** Only character deals damage

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I challenge with a character I just played?**
A: No. Characters are "drying" when played (after start of your turn) and can't challenge. Rush keyword allows this.

**Q: Can I challenge an Evasive character?**
A: Only if your character has Evasive or Alert. Normal characters can't challenge Evasive.

**Q: Do I have to challenge a Bodyguard character?**
A: Yes, if able. If opponent has Bodyguard character(s), you must choose one of them (unless Evasive prevents it).

**Q: Can I challenge a character with Ward?**
A: No. Ward prevents opponents from choosing that card when resolving effects, including challenges.

**Q: What happens if both characters would be banished?**
A: Both are banished. Damage happens simultaneously, so both triggers from leaving play would occur.

**Q: Can I challenge multiple times per turn?**
A: Yes. You can challenge multiple times with same or different characters, as long as they're dry and valid targets exist.

**Q: Does Rush let me quest on the turn I play the character?**
A: No. Rush only allows challenging. The character is still drying and can't quest or pay costs.

**Q: How does Resist work with damage modifiers?**
A: Resist reduces damage dealt. Other modifiers (Challenger, Support) increase Ⓒ, then Resist reduces final damage.
