# Section 1: Concepts

**Comprehensive Rules Version 2.0.1** - Rule References 1.1-1.12

## Overview

This section establishes the fundamental concepts of Disney Lorcana gameplay, including the golden rules that override other rules, player designations, card fundamentals, costs, abilities, timing, and core game mechanics.

---

## 1.1 General

**Rule refs:** 1.1.1-1.1.7

**Key Concepts:**

- Lorcana is played with two or more players, each with their own deck
- Players need a way to track lore totals and mark damage
- Tournament play has additional rules (see Tournament Rules)
- English cards and rules are the official versions for gameplay
- Card text and rules may be updated; most current information is official
- Multiplayer games have additional rules (Section 9)
- Reminder text in italics/parentheses is not rules text

---

## 1.2 Golden Rules

**Rule refs:** 1.2.1-1.2.3

The four golden rules supersede other game rules:

### 1.2.1 Card Text Overrides Rules

If a card's text contradicts a game rule, the card text wins.

**Example:** Mufasa - Betrayed Leader allows playing a character when banished, even though rule 4.3.2 says cards can only be played during the active player's turn.

### 1.2.2 Prohibition Beats Permission

If an effect prevents something, that prevention overrides rules/effects that allow it.

**Example:** Tiana - Celebrating Princess prevents playing actions while exerted with no cards in hand, even if an effect like Genie's would allow playing an action.

### 1.2.3 Do As Much As You Can

If an effect tells a player to do something, they do as much as possible even if some parts can't be done.

**Example:** Reflection says "Look at top 3 cards" - if deck has only 2 cards, look at those 2 and resolve normally.

## 1.3 Players

**Rule refs:** 1.3.1-1.3.6

**Key Concepts:**

- Each person playing is a player (minimum 2)
- Players have designations: active player, opponent, or teammate
- "You/your/yours" refers to player who played the card or generated the effect
- Designations can change during gameplay

### Active Player (1.3.4)

- Only one active player at a time
- Becomes active player when their turn starts
- Not referenced in card text, only for identifying whose turn it is

### Opponent (1.3.5)

- Anyone a player is playing against
- In multiplayer, a player has multiple opponents
- Can be referenced in card text as "opponent" or "opposing player"

### Teammate (1.3.6)

- Designated before game starts in some formats
- Teammates play together and aren't opponents to each other
- A player can't be their own teammate

## 1.4 Cards

**Rule refs:** 1.4.1-1.4.3.2

**Key Concepts:**

- A Lorcana deck contains only standard Disney Lorcana cards (2.5" x 3.5", Illuminarary icon on back
- Non-standard cards (different back, oversized, etc.) are not standard cards
- Cards in a player's deck are "their cards" - they make decisions unless otherwise specified
- Physical manipulation is done by the deck owner regardless of other specifications

### Card References (1.4.3.1-1.4.3.2)

- "You/your/yours" on card = player who played it
- "You/your/yours" in effect = player who generated it
- Pronouns (he/she/they/him/her/them/his/hers/theirs) refer to referenced characters/players

---

## 1.5 Costs

**Rule refs:** 1.5.1-1.5.5.4

**Key Concepts:**

- A cost is payment to play a card (ink cost in top left corner)
- Abilities may have costs that must be paid to use
- Costs must be paid in full
- Costs can't be changed, but payment modifiers can affect amount paid
- Some cards have alternate costs

### Alternate Costs (1.5.5)

- Singing a song (5.4.4.2)
- Shifting a character (8.10)
- Playing "for free" means without paying ink cost (6.1.7)
- Using ability "for free" means without paying costs except ○ (6.1.7.1)

---

## 1.6 Abilities

**Rule refs:** 1.6.1-1.6.3

**Key Concepts:**

- Abilities normally apply only when source is in play
- Source is normally the card or effect generating it

### Ability Types

- **Triggered abilities (1.6.1.1)** - Continuously look for condition, have effect when met
- **Activated abilities (1.6.1.2)** - Player chooses to use, requires cost payment
- **Static abilities (1.6.1.3)** - Always active while source is in play

---

## 1.7 Game Actions, Timing, and Illegal Actions

**Rule refs:** 1.7.1-1.7.7

**Key Concepts:**

- Game actions are specific actions players can take
- Turn actions are subset available during Main Phase
- Actions have restrictions; illegal actions can't be taken

### Game Actions

- Drawing a card (1.7.2)
- Inking a card (1.7.3)
- Playing a card (1.7.4)
- Activating an ability (1.7.5)
- Questing (1.7.6)
- Challenging (1.7.7)

### Turn Actions

Actions available during Main Phase:

- Ink a card
- Play a card
- Use an activated ability
- Quest with a character
- Challenge with a character
- Move a character to a location

## 1.8 Game State Check

**Rule refs:** 1.8.1-1.8.2

**Key Concepts:**

- Happens after every action/ability resolves
- Checks for win/loss conditions
- Checks for damage-based banishment

### What's Checked (1.8.1)

1. **Win condition:** Any player reached 20 or more lore
2. **Loss condition:** A player has tried to draw from empty deck this turn
3. **Damage check:** Characters/locations with damage ≥ willpower are banished

### Triggered Abilities (1.8.2)

- Triggered abilities from game state check are added to bag after check completes

---

## 1.9 Damage

**Rule refs:** 1.9.1-1.9.5

**Key Concepts:**

- Damage is represented by damage counters on cards
- Damage counters can be "put" (counters) or "dealt" (modified by effects)
- Damage on a card reduces effective willpower
- Damage is removed when card leaves play

### Damage Representation (1.9.2)

- Single damage counter = 1 damage
- Counters for more than 1 damage count as separate counters per 1 damage

### Dealing Damage (1.9.4)

- Damage dealt can be modified by effects
- Final damage is what's actually dealt

### Putting Damage (1.9.5)

- Putting damage counters is NOT damage dealt
- Not affected by damage modifiers
- Directly applies to card's willpower

---

## 1.10 Materials

**Rule refs:** 1.10.1-1.10.3

**Key Concepts:**

- Players need Disney Lorcana cards to play
- Need method to track lore (paper, tokens, dice, etc.)
- Need method to track damage (counters, dice, etc.)

---

## 1.11 Lore

**Rule refs:** 1.11.1-1.11.3

**Key Concepts:**

- Lore is the resource players race to gain
- First player to reach 20 lore wins
- Lore is gained by questing with characters or from locations

### Winning the Game (1.11.2)

- When game state check finds player with 20+ lore, that player wins
- Game ends immediately

### Losing the Game (1.11.3)

- If a player tries to draw from empty deck, they will lose at end of turn
- Loss condition is checked during game state check

---

## 1.12 Drawing

**Rule refs:** 1.12.1-1.12.4

**Key Concepts:**

- Drawing is taking top card of deck and putting it into hand
- Can only draw from own deck
- Drawing 0 cards is possible (does nothing)
