# Section 4: Turn Actions

**Comprehensive Rules Version 2.0.1** - Rule References 4.1-4.7

## Overview

This section details each turn action available during the Main Phase: Inking a card, Playing a card, Using an activated ability, Questing, Challenging, and Moving a character to a location.

---

## 4.1 General

**Rule refs:** 4.1.1-4.1.2

**Key Concepts:**

- Turn actions are taken during Main Phase
- Active player may take any number of turn actions in any order
- Each turn action must complete before next begins
- Turn actions have specific restrictions and requirements

### Turn Action Types (4.1.1)

1. Ink a card
2. Play a card
3. Use an activated ability
4. Quest
5. Challenge
6. Move a character to a location

### Action Requirements (4.1.2)

- Must follow all rules for that action type
- Must have required resources (ink, dry character, etc.)
- Can't take illegal actions

---

## 4.2 Ink a Card

**Rule refs:** 4.2.1-4.2.4

Inking puts a card from hand into inkwell facedown as ink resource.

### 4.2.1 General

- Can ink only 1 card per turn
- Card must have inkwell symbol (○)
- Can't ink during opponent's turn

### 4.2.2 To Ink a Card

1. Choose card from hand with inkwell symbol
2. Put that card into inkwell facedown and ready

### 4.2.3 Additional Ink

Some effects allow adding additional cards to inkwell:

- Announce using effect
- Follow normal inking steps
- Counts as separate instance

### 4.2.4 Ink from Effects

Cards put into inkwell by effects:

- Not revealed
- Not required to have inkwell symbol
- Enter inkwell facedown

---

## 4.3 Play a Card

**Rule refs:** 4.3.1-4.3.7

Playing puts a card from hand into play.

### 4.3.1 General

- Can play only during your turn (unless effect says otherwise)
- Must pay cost (ink cost or alternate cost)
- Card type determines how it enters play

### 4.3.2 Playing Process

1. **Announce** - Reveal card from hand
2. **Choose Zone** - For characters, choose which zone to play into
3. **Pay Cost** - Pay ink cost or alternate cost
4. **Put into Play** - Card is now "played"

### 4.3.3 Resolving Cards

After being "played":

**Characters/Items/Locations (4.3.3.1):**

- Enter Play zone
- If Shift, put on top of card with same name

**Actions (4.3.3.2):**

- Enter Play zone
- Effect resolves immediately
- Then put into discard pile

### 4.3.4 Timing

- Can't play during opponent's turn (unless card allows)
- Can't play if can't pay cost
- Effects that say "play for free" bypass ink cost

### 4.3.5 Playing Songs

Songs are actions with additional cost options:

- Pay ink cost normally, OR
- Exert character(s) to sing (see Singer, Sing Together keywords)

### 4.3.6 Restrictions

Can't play if:

- Cost can't be paid
- Card isn't in hand
- It's opponent's turn (unless card says otherwise)

### 4.3.7 Zone Choice

For characters entering play:

- Choose which zone to enter (normally your Play zone)
- Effects can specify different zone

---

## 4.4 Use an Activated Ability

**Rule refs:** 4.4.1-4.4.5

Using an activated ability means paying its cost to get its effect.

### 4.4.1 General

- Can use only during your turn (while no effects resolving)
- Character can't be questing or in challenge
- Must be able to pay all costs

### 4.4.2 Activated Ability Costs

Costs may include:

- ○ (exerting the character)
- Paying ink
- Other payments specified by ability

**For Free Abilities:**

- "Use for free" = no cost except ○ (6.1.7.1)
- ○ must still be paid if required

### 4.4.3 Character Restrictions

- **With ○ cost:** Character must be dry
- **Without ○ cost:** Can use same turn character enters play

### 4.4.4 Item/Location Abilities

- Can use same turn item/location enters play

### 4.4.5 Process

1. Announce ability being used
2. Pay all costs
3. Ability effect resolves
4. Resulting triggers go into bag

---

## 4.5 Quest

**Rule refs:** 4.5.1-4.5.6

Questing exerts a character to gain lore.

### 4.5.1 General

- Exert character to gain lore equal to its ◇ value
- Can't quest same turn character enters play (drying character)
- Can't quest if already exerted

### 4.5.2 Restrictions

Can't quest if character is:

- Exerted
- Drying (played after start of turn)
- In a challenge

### 4.5.3 Process

1. Choose dry character
2. Exert that character
3. Gain lore equal to character's ◇ value

### 4.5.4 Triggered Abilities

- "Whenever this character quests" abilities trigger
- Added to bag, resolve after quest completes

### 4.5.5 Negative Lore

If character has negative ◇:

- Counts as 0 for lore gained
- Actual value unchanged (still negative)

### 4.5.6 Support Keyword

Characters with Support can add ◇ to another character when questing.

---

## 4.6 Challenge

**Rule refs:** 4.6.1-4.6.8

Challenging is combat between characters or against a location.

### 4.6.1 General

- Exert your dry character to fight
- Choose exerted opposing character OR opposing location
- Each deals damage equal to their Ⓒ to the other

### 4.6.2 Restrictions

**Challenging Character:**

- Must be dry
- Can't be already exerted
- Must be able to challenge target

**Challenged Character:**

- Must be exerted (opponent's character)
- OR must be a location

**Targeting Restrictions:**

- Evasive: Can't be challenged except by Evasive
- Bodyguard: Must challenge this if able
- Ward: Can't be chosen by opponent

### 4.6.3 Process

1. **Declare:** Choose challenging and challenged character/location
2. **Challenge Damage Step:** Each deals Ⓒ damage simultaneously
3. **Damage Resolution:** Apply damage modifiers and deal damage
4. **Triggers:** Resulting abilities go into bag

### 4.6.4 Damage Calculation

- Challenging character deals Ⓒ damage to challenged
- Challenged character deals Ⓒ damage to challenging
- For locations, only challenging character deals damage

### 4.6.5 Keyword Interactions

- **Challenger +N:** Gains +N Ⓒ while challenging
- **Evasive:** Can't be challenged except by Evasive (Alert ignores this)
- **Rush:** Can challenge as though dry (even when drying)
- **Reckless:** Can't end turn if ready and can challenge

### 4.6.6 Location Challenges

When challenging a location:

- Only challenging character deals damage
- Location has ⣇ (willpower) like characters
- Location banished when damage ≥ ⣇

### 4.6.7 Multiple Challenges

- Same character can challenge multiple times (if ready and exerted targets exist)
- Each challenge is separate turn action

### 4.6.8 Support

Support keyword allows adding Ⓒ during challenge damage step.

---

## 4.7 Move a Character

**Rule refs:** 4.7.1-4.7.4

Moving sends a character from one location to another.

### 4.7.1 General

- Pay move cost to move character to location you control
- Character doesn't exert or ready
- Can move regardless of ready/exerted state

### 4.7.2 Move Cost

- Each location has move cost
- Must pay full move cost to move
- Costs can be modified by effects

### 4.7.3 Process

1. Choose character you control
2. Choose location you control
3. Pay move cost
4. Character is now at that location

### 4.7.4 Restrictions

- Must control both character and destination location
- Must be able to pay move cost
- Some locations have restrictions on what can move there

## Quick Reference

| Action                    | Key Restriction                                               | Effect                                |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Ink a card**            | 1 per turn, card must have ○                                  | Gain ink resource                     |
| **Play a card**           | During your turn, pay cost                                    | Card enters play (or action resolves) |
| **Use activated ability** | Character must be dry (for ○ cost), not questing/in challenge | Ability effect resolves               |
| **Quest**                 | Character must be dry, not exerted                            | Gain lore equal to ◇                  |
| **Challenge**             | Challenger dry, challenged exerted                            | Both deal Ⓒ damage to each other      |
| **Move character**        | Pay move cost, control destination                            | Character changes location            |
