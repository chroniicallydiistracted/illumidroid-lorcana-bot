# Disney Lorcana Glossary

Reference for the terms, phrases, and shorthand used in the Madrigal deck guides and normal Disney Lorcana play.

Primary rules source used: **Disney Lorcana Comprehensive Rules, Version 2.1.0, effective April 30, 2026**, plus the official Disney Lorcana Resources page.

This glossary separates three things:

1. **Official gameplay terms**: terms used by the rules or printed cards.
2. **Card/deck construction terms**: terms used when building and importing decks from deck building websites.
3. **Strategy shorthand**: terms players use when discussing gameplay, matchups, and decisions.

For tournament disputes, the current official comprehensive rules and tournament documents have the current exact ruling.

---

# 1. Core game goal

## Lore

The score you are trying to reach. A player wins by reaching **20 or more lore**, unless another game-ending condition applies.

## Gain lore

To increase your lore total. Characters normally gain lore by questing. Locations may gain lore during the start of your turn. Card effects may also cause lore gain.

## Lore total

The current amount of lore a player has.

## Race

A game state where both players are trying to reach 20 lore faster than the other, often without fully controlling the opposing board.

## Lore race

A race focused on comparing how much lore each player can gain over the next one or two turns.

## Lethal

A strategy term, not an official Lorcana keyword. It means a player has a line of play that can reach 20 or more lore immediately or before the opponent can stop it.

---

# 2. Players and turn identity

## Player

A person playing the game.

## Active player

The player whose turn it currently is.

## Opponent / opposing player

A player you are playing against.

## You / your

On a card or effect, this refers to the player who played the card or generated the effect.

## Starting player

The player who takes the first turn of the game.

## Turn order

The order in which players take turns.

## Play/draw rule

In a best-of series, the loser of the previous game chooses whether to go first or not in the next game.

---

# 3. Game setup terms

## Opening hand

The first 7 cards a player draws at the start of the game.

## Mulligan

Common player shorthand for **altering your starting hand**. Lorcana’s official term is not “mulligan,” but players commonly use it.

## Alter a starting hand

At the start of the game, after drawing 7 cards, a player may place any number of cards from hand on the bottom of their deck without revealing them, draw back up to 7, then shuffle if they altered by at least one card. This can be done only once per game.

## Shuffle

Randomize the order of cards in a deck.

## Cut

A player divides and re-stacks a deck after it has been shuffled, usually as a final randomization check.

## Concede

To voluntarily lose the game.

---

# 4. Deck construction terms

## Deck

The cards a player uses to play the game. In Core Constructed, a deck has 60 or more cards, up to two ink types, and no more than four copies of the same full card name.

## Constructed

A format where players bring a deck they built before the event or game.

## Limited

A format where players build decks from cards opened or provided during the event.

## Core Constructed

The main constructed Lorcana format. The important deck-building rules are 60+ cards, no more than two ink types, and no more than four copies of the same full card name.

## Legal

Allowed in the format being played.

## Illegal

Not allowed in the format being played.

## Rotation

A scheduled change where older sets stop being legal in a format.

## Set

A product release group of cards, such as Fabled, Whispers in the Well, Winterspell, or Wilds Unknown.

## Card pool

All cards available for deckbuilding under the chosen rules.

## Full name

For a character or location, the card’s name and version together. Example: `Isabela Madrigal - Caring Cultivator`.

## Copy

One physical or digital instance of a card in a deck. In Core Constructed, you may play up to four copies of the same full card name.

## Ink type

A card’s color identity. The six ink types are Amber, Amethyst, Emerald, Ruby, Sapphire, and Steel.

## Two-color deck

A deck using cards from no more than two ink types.

## Mono-color deck

A deck using one ink type.

## Splash

A small number of cards from a secondary ink type. In Lorcana, a deck can use up to two ink types total.

## Import list

A plain text list of card quantities and names used by deckbuilding websites or online play tools.

## Practice bot deck

A deck built to be piloted by an automated or testing opponent to help you practice a matchup.

# 5. Ink and resource terms

## Ink

The resource used to pay costs. Cards in your inkwell represent ink.

## Ink card

A card in your inkwell.

## Inkwell

The private zone where ink cards are placed facedown. Each card in the inkwell represents 1 ink. Players may count cards in an inkwell but cannot look at the fronts of cards in any inkwell.

## Inkable

A card with the inkwell symbol around its cost. It can be used for the normal once-per-turn ink action.

## Uninkable

A card without the inkwell symbol around its cost. It cannot be used for the normal once-per-turn ink action unless another effect specifically allows it.

## Ink a card / inking

The turn action of revealing an inkable card from hand, verifying it has the inkwell symbol, then putting it into your inkwell facedown and ready. Normally this can be done once per turn.

## Put into the inkwell

A broader phrase. If an effect puts a card into the inkwell, it may not follow the normal ink action process unless the effect says so.

## Ready ink

Ink cards in your inkwell that are upright and available to exert for payment.

## Exerted ink

Ink cards in your inkwell that have been turned sideways to pay for something. They ready during the Ready step.

## Ramp

Strategy shorthand for increasing available ink faster than normal, usually by putting extra cards into the inkwell.

## Curve

The sequence of plays your deck can make across turns based on ink cost. A good curve means you can spend your ink efficiently each turn.

## On curve

Playing a card that uses your available ink efficiently for that turn, such as a 3-cost card on turn 3.

## Off curve

A turn where your available ink is not used efficiently, usually because your hand does not contain an appropriate-cost card.

# 6. Card types

## Card

A standard Disney Lorcana card.

## Character

A card type that enters play and can usually quest, challenge, sing, use abilities, be challenged, take damage, and be banished.

## Action

A card type that gives a one-time effect. After resolving, it goes to discard.

## Song

A subtype of action. Songs can be played normally by paying ink, or sung by exerting a ready character with enough cost or an ability allowing them to sing.

## Item

A card type that enters play and remains until banished or otherwise removed. Items can use activated abilities the turn they are played if the cost can be paid.

## Location

A card type that enters play and can be moved to by characters. Locations have willpower, can take damage, can be challenged, and may generate lore during the start of your turn.

## Classification

Words on a card’s classification line, such as Hero, Ally, Princess, Madrigal, Dreamborn, Storyborn, or Floodborn. Some effects care about classifications.

## Madrigal

A classification used by Encanto-family character cards. In the deck guide, “Madrigal character” means a character card with the Madrigal classification.

## Storyborn

A classification usually indicating a version close to the original story identity of that character.

## Dreamborn

A classification usually indicating a reimagined or alternate version of a character.

## Floodborn

A classification often tied to Shift characters and transformed versions.

## Version

The subtitle after the dash in a card’s full name. Example: in `Bruno Madrigal - Oasis Oracle`, `Oasis Oracle` is the version.

---

# 7. Card stats and symbols

## Ink cost

The amount of ink needed to play a card, shown in the top-left corner.

## Strength

A character stat. It determines how much damage that character deals in a challenge.

## Willpower

A durability stat. If a character or location has damage equal to or greater than its willpower, it is banished.

## Lore value

A character or location stat showing how much lore it can gain through its normal lore-gaining method. Characters gain that lore by questing. Locations with a lore value gain lore during the start of your turn.

## Move cost

The cost to move one of your characters to one of your locations.

## Exert symbol

The symbol on abilities or songs that means the card or character must be exerted as part of the cost.

## Inkwell symbol

The symbol around a card’s ink cost that means the card can be inked by the normal once-per-turn ink action.

---

# 8. Zones

## Zone

A game area where cards can exist.

## Deck

A private zone containing the cards you draw from.

## Hand

A private zone containing cards you have drawn but not played or inked. Players may count the number of cards in any hand, but cannot look at an opponent’s hand unless an effect allows it.

## Play zone

The public zone where characters, items, and locations exist after being played. Actions are briefly in play while resolving, then go to discard.

## Inkwell

The private zone where ink cards are kept facedown.

## Discard / discard pile

The public faceup pile where actions go after resolving and where cards usually go after being discarded or banished.

## Bag

A non-physical zone where triggered abilities wait before resolving.

## Public zone

A zone where card identities are normally known to all players.

## Private zone

A zone where card identities are normally hidden from other players.

---

# 9. Card states

## Ready

Upright. A ready card can be exerted if a rule or cost allows it.

## Exerted

Sideways. An exerted character can usually be challenged. Exerted cards ready during their controller’s Ready step.

## Dry

A character that was in play at the start of its player’s current turn. Dry characters can quest, challenge, sing, and pay exert costs if otherwise allowed.

## Drying

A character played after the start of its player’s current turn. Drying characters cannot quest, challenge, or pay costs requiring them to exert. Their passive or triggered abilities still function unless a rule or effect says otherwise.

## Damaged

A character or location with one or more damage on it.

## Undamaged

A character or location with no damage on it.

## Faceup

A card whose front is visible.

## Facedown

A card whose back is visible and front is hidden.

## In play

A faceup card in the Play zone with no card on top of it. A card under another card is not considered in play.

## Under

A card beneath another card in a stack. It is not considered in play.

## On top

A card with one or more cards under it.

## Stack

A group of cards on top of or under one another in play, commonly created by Shift or Boost.

---

# 10. Turn structure

## Turn

One player’s complete sequence of Start-of-Turn Phase, Main Phase, and End-of-Turn Phase.

## Start-of-Turn Phase

The first phase of a turn. It has the Ready step, Set step, and Draw step.

## Ready step

The step where the active player readies exerted cards.

## Set step

The step where start-of-turn effects and location lore can happen.

## Draw step

The step where the active player draws a card. The starting player skips this step on the first turn of the game.

## Main Phase

The phase where the active player can take turn actions in any legal order.

## End-of-Turn Phase

The phase where end-of-turn effects happen and the turn closes.

---

# 11. Turn actions

## Turn action

An action the game allows the active player to take during the Main Phase. Examples include inking a card, playing a card, questing, challenging, moving a character, and using activated abilities.

## Play a card

Reveal the card from hand, pay its cost or alternate cost, and resolve the appropriate process. Characters, items, and locations enter play. Actions resolve and then go to discard.

## Use an activated ability

Pay an ability’s cost to get its effect.

## Quest

Exert a dry character to gain lore equal to that character’s lore value.

## Challenge

Use one dry ready character to attack an opposing exerted character or a location, if legal. Both characters deal damage equal to their strength during the challenge damage step. Locations do not deal challenge damage back.

## Move a character

Pay a location’s move cost to move one of your characters to that location.

## Sing

Use a ready dry character to pay the alternate cost of a song. The character must meet the song’s cost requirement unless an ability changes that.

---

# 12. Costs and payment

## Cost

Something that must be paid to play a card or use an ability.

## Ink cost

The normal printed cost of a card.

## Alternate cost

A different cost allowed by a rule or effect instead of paying normal ink cost. Singing a song and shifting a character are alternate costs.

## For free

Playing a card or using an ability without paying its normal cost. It is still treated as an alternate cost permission.

## Payment modifier

An effect that changes how much must be paid without changing the card’s printed cost.

## Can’t pay

If a full cost cannot be paid, the card cannot be played or the ability cannot be used.

---

# 13. Abilities and effects

## Ability

Text that gives a card or effect some game function.

## Effect

The result of resolving card text, an ability, or a rule instruction.

## Triggered ability

An ability that starts with timing language such as “when,” “whenever,” “at the start of,” or “at the end of.” When its condition happens, it is added to the bag to resolve.

## Activated ability

An ability a player may use by paying its cost.

## Static ability

An ability that continuously changes characteristics, rules, or game state for as long as its duration applies.

## Replacement effect

An effect that changes what would happen into something else.

## Delayed triggered ability

A triggered ability created now that waits to trigger at a later specified moment.

## Source

The card or effect that generated an ability or effect.

## Resolve

Carry out an effect’s instructions.

## Do as much as possible

If an effect cannot fully happen, perform every part that can legally happen.

## Choose / chosen

A card or player is selected as part of resolving an effect. Ward prevents opponents from choosing that card when resolving an effect.

## Target

Common strategy shorthand for a chosen card or player. Lorcana card text usually says “chosen,” not “target.”

## May

Optional. If an effect says “may,” the player can choose not to do it.

## Must

Required if legal and possible.

## Reveal

Show a hidden card so all players can see it.

## Look at

View a hidden card or cards. Unless the effect says to reveal them, other players do not see them.

## Put into hand

Move a card into a player’s hand.

## Put on top

Place a card on top of a deck or stack, as instructed.

## Put on bottom

Place a card on the bottom of a deck, as instructed.

## In any order

The player performing the instruction chooses the order.

---

# 14. Damage and banishing

## Damage

Counters placed on a character or location to track harm. If damage equals or exceeds willpower, the card is banished.

## Deal damage

An effect or challenge causes damage to be dealt.

## Put damage

Place damage counters directly on a character or location. This is not the same as damage being dealt if the rules or effect distinguish them.

## Move damage

Transfer damage from one character or location to another as instructed. Damage moved this way is not damage being dealt.

## Remove damage / heal

Take damage counters off a character or location. “Heal” is common shorthand for removing damage.

## Take damage

A broad phrase meaning damage was dealt, put, or moved onto a character or location.

## Banish

Move a character, item, or location from play to its player’s discard pile because of an effect or because damage equals or exceeds willpower.

## Banished in a challenge

A character was banished during the challenge process.

## Leaves play

A card moves from the Play zone to another zone.

## Removed from play

A card leaves the Play zone because of a rule or effect.

---

# 15. Keywords

## Keyword

A short named ability or ability package. Reminder text on cards helps memory, but the full rules determine how the keyword works.

## Alert

A character with Alert ignores the challenge restriction created by Evasive. It does not itself become Evasive.

## Bodyguard

A character with Bodyguard may enter play exerted. While it is a legal challenge choice, opponents must challenge a character with Bodyguard instead of another character you control, if able.

## Boost N

Once during your turn, you may pay N ink to put the top card of your deck facedown under the card with Boost.

## Challenger +N

While this character is challenging, it gets +N strength. It does not get that bonus while being challenged.

## Evasive

This card can be challenged only by a character with Evasive, unless another ability such as Alert changes that restriction.

## Reckless

A character with Reckless cannot quest. If it is ready and able to challenge, its controller cannot end their turn without challenging with it or otherwise making it unable to challenge.

## Resist +N

Damage that would be dealt to this character or location is reduced by N. Resist reduces dealt damage; it does not reduce damage that is put or moved.

## Rush

This character can challenge as though it had been in play since the beginning of your turn. Rush does not allow the character to quest immediately.

## Shift N

If you have a character in play with the same name, you may pay the Shift cost instead of the normal ink cost and put the Shift character on top of that character.

## Classification Shift

A Shift variant that lets you shift onto a character with the specified classification rather than the same name.

## Universal Shift

A Shift variant that lets you shift onto another character you have in play, as specified by the card.

## Singer N

This character can exert to sing a song as though it had cost N. Its actual ink cost does not change.

## Sing Together N

Instead of paying a song’s ink cost, you can exert one or more ready characters whose combined costs are N or more to play that song.

## Support

Whenever this character quests, you may add its strength to another chosen character’s strength this turn.

## Vanish

When this character is chosen by an opponent while resolving an action’s effect, it is banished after that action resolves unless it has already moved zones.

## Ward

Opponents cannot choose this card while resolving effects. Effects that do not choose can still affect it.

# 16. Card text phrases

## When you play this character

A triggered ability that happens after the character is played.

## Whenever this character quests

A triggered ability that happens when the character quests.

## At the start of your turn

A triggered ability or effect timing that happens during the start of your turn, usually in the Start-of-Turn Phase.

## At the end of your turn

A triggered ability or effect timing that happens during your End-of-Turn Phase.

## This turn

The effect lasts until the current turn ends.

## Until the start of your next turn

The effect lasts through the opponent’s turn and ends at the start of your next turn.

## During your turn

The effect applies only during the turns where you are the active player.

## Chosen character

A character selected by the player resolving the effect.

## Chosen opposing character

A character controlled by an opponent selected by the player resolving the effect.

## Another chosen character

A chosen character that is not the source character itself.

## Ready chosen character

Put the chosen character into the ready position.

## They cannot quest or challenge this turn

A restriction preventing that character from questing or challenging for the stated duration.

## Must quest if able

A restriction requiring the character to quest if it legally can.

## Cannot challenge

A restriction preventing a character from being declared as a challenger.

## Cannot be challenged

A restriction preventing a card from being selected as the object of a challenge.

## Enters play

A card is placed into the Play zone.

## Enters play exerted

The card is put into play sideways rather than ready.

## Return to hand

Move the card to its player’s hand.

## Return from discard

Move a card from discard to another zone, usually hand.

## Draw a card

Take the top card of your deck and put it into your hand.

## Discard a card

Choose a card from your hand and put it into your discard pile.

## Banish chosen item

Move the chosen item from play to its player’s discard pile.

## Banish chosen character

Move the chosen character from play to its player’s discard pile.

---

# 17. Location-specific terms

## Location lore

Lore a location gains during the start of its controller’s turn if it has a lore value.

## Move to a location

Pay a location’s move cost and place one of your characters at that location.

## At a location

A character is currently placed at a location.

## Challenge a location

A legal challenge against a location. The challenging character deals damage to the location. The location does not deal challenge damage back.

## Location willpower

How much damage a location can take before being banished.

# 19. Strategy archetypes

## Aggro

A strategy that tries to win quickly with early characters and fast lore gain.

## Control

A strategy that tries to slow the game, answer threats, and win later with stronger cards or inevitability.

## Midrange

A strategy between aggro and control. It plays efficient characters and flexible answers, then shifts between attacking and defending based on the game state.

## Tempo

A strategy that tries to stay ahead by making efficient plays that pressure the opponent while disrupting their development.

## Evasive deck

A deck built around Evasive characters. It wins by questing with characters that many opposing characters cannot challenge.

## Location deck

A deck that gains advantage through locations, location synergies, or forcing opponents to attack locations.

## Meta

The field of commonly played and successful decks in a given environment.

## Matchup

How one deck tends to play against another deck.

## Favorable matchup

A matchup where your deck is expected to have an advantage if piloted correctly.

## Unfavorable matchup

A matchup where the opposing deck attacks your weaknesses or is expected to have an advantage.

## Tech card

A card included mainly to improve a specific matchup or problem.

## Flex slot

A deck slot that can be changed without damaging the main engine.

## Greedy

A strategy or build that maximizes power or payoff but may be weaker to fast pressure, bad draws, or disruption.

## Under-teched

A deck that does not include enough answers for a known problem.

# 20. Tactical strategy phrases

## Board

The cards a player has in play.

## Board state

The complete current arrangement of cards in play, plus relevant lore, hand sizes, damage, and available ink.

## Board control

The ability to manage or reduce the opponent’s characters, items, and locations.

## Clear the board

Remove most or all opposing threats from play. The Madrigal deck usually should not try to clear every Evasive character.

## Threat

A card that can create a major problem if left unanswered.

## Must-answer threat

A card that will likely win the game or create overwhelming advantage unless answered.

## Clock

How quickly a player can win if the board state continues unchanged.

## Two-turn clock

A board or lore state where a player can likely win within two turns.

## Race line

A planned sequence focused on reaching 20 lore before the opponent, rather than controlling every threat.

## Control line

A planned sequence focused on removing threats and stabilizing before trying to win.

## Stabilize

Reach a board state where you are no longer in immediate danger of losing.

## Recovery turn

A turn spent rebuilding after falling behind.

## Value

Extra benefit beyond the basic body or effect of a card.

## Card advantage

Having more useful cards available than the opponent, usually through drawing cards, making multiple opposing cards answer one of yours, or preserving hand size.

## Card selection

Improving card quality without necessarily gaining extra cards, usually by looking at cards and choosing which to keep.

## Cantrip

A card that replaces itself by drawing a card while doing something else.

## Filter

Look at or manipulate cards to improve draw quality.

## Dig

Use card selection or draw to search for a needed card.

## Topdeck

The card drawn from the top of the deck, especially when a player has few or no cards in hand.

## Dead card

A card that has little or no useful function in the current matchup or board state.

## Live card

A card that is useful in the current board state or matchup.

## Line / line of play

A planned sequence of actions.

## Sequencing

The order in which you perform actions. In Lorcana, sequencing often matters because triggers and payoffs may need to be in play before healing, questing, or playing characters.

## Curve out

Play useful cards on consecutive turns while spending ink efficiently.

## Brick

Draw cards that do not let you make useful plays.

## Flood

Draw too many cards that only serve as ink or too many high-cost cards relative to the game state.

## Gas

Useful cards remaining in hand or deck that keep pressure going.

## Empty hand

Having no cards in hand. This is dangerous because you have few options unless your board or draw engine is already strong.

## Overextend

Play too many cards into a situation where the opponent can punish you with removal, bounce, or board wipe effects.

## Hold

Choose not to play a card yet.

## Commit

Put a card or resource into play or into a line of play.

## Bait

Play or expose something to force the opponent to spend an answer before you play your more important card.

## Punish

Take advantage of an opponent’s mistake or overcommitment.

## Trade

A sequence where both players lose cards, often through challenges.

## Favorable trade

A trade where you lose less value than the opponent.

## Bad trade

A trade where you lose more value than the opponent or give up your win condition.

## Race math

Counting how much lore each player can gain over the next turns to decide whether to quest, challenge, or remove a threat.

## Lethal math

Counting whether you can reach 20 lore this turn or next turn.

# 21. Common decision terms

## Keep

During the starting-hand decision, retain a card instead of putting it on the bottom of the deck.

## Ship / ship back

Player slang for putting a card from the opening hand on the bottom of the deck while altering the starting hand.

## Hard keep

A card you strongly want to keep in the opening hand for a matchup.

## Usually keep

A card that is good to keep only if the rest of the hand is functional.

## Usually ship

A card that is normally too slow, too situational, or too redundant for the opening hand.

## Default line

The normal play sequence when no unusual board state changes your priorities.

## Priority target

The opposing card you should answer before other cards.

## Ignore

Choose not to answer a card because it is less important than racing, developing, or answering a bigger threat.

## Answer

A card or play that removes, neutralizes, or otherwise handles an opposing threat.

## Neutralize

Stop a threat from mattering without necessarily banishing it.

## Protect

Prevent an important card from being banished, chosen, or profitably challenged.

## Pressure

Force the opponent to respond because your lore gain or board will otherwise win.

## Passive pressure

Lore or advantage created without needing risky character challenges. Locations and repeatable items can create passive pressure.

## Quest pressure

Threatening to gain lore by questing with characters.

## Location pressure

Threatening lore or value through locations.

## Removal

Cards or effects that banish, return, move, exert, or otherwise answer opposing cards.

## Hard removal

Removal that cleanly answers a character regardless of damage or challenge math, such as putting it into the inkwell or banishing it directly.

## Soft removal

An effect that delays, restricts, exerts, bounces, or otherwise weakens a threat without permanently eliminating it.

## Bounce

Return a card from play to hand.

## Disruption

Effects that interfere with the opponent’s plan, such as removing a character, forcing a discard, exerting a character, or restricting challenges.

# 22. Common Lorcana mistakes tied to terms

## Inking every turn

Not required. Inking is normally once per turn, not mandatory. If your hand is low and you already have enough ink, holding cards may be better.

## Healing too early

Removing damage before Bruno or Caring Cultivator is active may waste future value unless the character needs to survive.

## Challenging because a target exists

A legal challenge is not automatically a good challenge. Compare the lore gained by questing against the lore denied by challenging.

## Confusing Evasive and Ward

Evasive restricts who can challenge the card. Ward restricts opponents from choosing the card with effects. They protect against different things.

## Confusing Resist with healing

Resist reduces damage as it would be dealt. Healing removes damage already on a card. Resist does not reduce damage that is put or moved.

## Confusing dry and ready

Ready means upright. Dry means the character has been in play since the start of your turn. A ready but drying character still cannot quest, challenge, or pay exert costs.

## Confusing name and full name

Card effects that refer to a character name usually care about the name line, not the version. Deck construction copy limits care about full name.

## Thinking Shift keeps old text

A shifted character does not gain the text of the card underneath it, but it keeps relevant states such as damage and whether it was dry or exerted.

# 24. Minimal table-side definitions

## Ink

Resource.

## Inkwell

Where resource cards go facedown.

## Quest

Exert a dry character to gain lore.

## Challenge

Use a dry ready character to attack an opposing exerted character or location.

## Dry

Able to quest, challenge, sing, or pay exert costs because it was already in play at the start of your turn.

## Drying

Newly played this turn; cannot quest, challenge, or pay exert costs yet.

## Evasive

Can be challenged only by Evasive characters unless another ability changes that.

## Ward

Cannot be chosen by opponents’ effects.

## Resist

Reduces damage dealt.

## Shift

Play a shift character on top of another eligible character for its Shift cost.

## Singer

Counts as a higher cost only for singing songs.

## Support

When questing, adds its strength to another chosen character this turn.

## Banish

Put a card from play into discard.

## Lore

Score. Reach 20 to win.
